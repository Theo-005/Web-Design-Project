Intro

Urbanization is expanding rapidly, with cities expected to house nearly 70% of the worldâ€™s population by 2050. However, this growth comes with serious challenges, including rising poverty, inadequate housing, strained transport systems, increasing vulnerability to disasters and increasing carbon emissions being released into the environment. Achieving Sustainable Development Goal 11 requires integrated urban strategies that promote affordable housing, inclusive planning, cultural heritage protection and reduced effect on the environment. Global initiatives such as the Local2030 Coalition are helping drive progress by coordinating efforts among governments, UN agencies and other partners.



Recent assessments of specific Goal 11 targets show mixed progress. Housing affordability remains a major global issue, with up to 3 billion people affected and significant regional cost disparities. Funding for world heritage protection, though slightly increased, stays far below what is needed, especially in developing countries. Disasters continue to cause extensive damage to infrastructure and essential services each year. Disaster risk reduction has strengthened, however, with most local governments now implementing dedicated strategies.

Preview

In exploring sustainable urban development, we examined how cities can advance Goal 11 through clean travel, low-carbon emissions, clean energy and stronger protection of cultural identity. Our research highlighted the growing importance of clean travel systems, renewable energy and reducing urban carbon emissions, particularly as urban populations continue to rise. We also looked at efforts to safeguard heritage sites, ensuring that modernization does not come at the expense of cultural preservation. Together, these focus areas show how integrated, forward-looking urban policies can create cities and communities that are not only more sustainable and energy-efficient but also inclusive, connected, and deeply rooted in their historical and cultural contexts.
